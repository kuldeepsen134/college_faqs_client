What is a Master of Business Administration (MBA)?

MBA is a two-year full-time postgraduate degree that encompasses a broad range of general management and business strategies focusing on real-world problems. Pursuing an MBA program can uplift your career and skills, and also gives you a lot of opportunities to increase your salary and career growth. To secure a seat in a top B-school, a student needs to appear for different entrance exams such as CAT, XAT, SNAP, NMAT, GMAT etc. This is followed by Group Discussion (GD), Written Aptitude Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds.

Types of MBA

MBA programs will vary between disciplines, specialties, and colleges. Traditionally, full-time MBA of two years duration has been the popular choice among management aspirants, however over the last few years needs and time and have changed and other formats have cropped up and have gained immense popularity among aspirants owning to flexibility.

Full-Time MBA: One of the most common types of MBA programs is a two-year commitment in which candidates attend college full-time. During the first year of the program, MBA candidates may learn more fundamental business skills such as strategy or communication and learn specific electives in the second year.

Part-Time MBA: Students not looking to leave work and willing to attend college for a longer time may pursue a part-time MBA program. This type of program often has greater flexibility on how many courses can be taken at once and how quickly a student must move through the MBA program. Part-time programs may be more favorable for candidates wanting to study at their own pace.

Distance MBA: In Distance MBA the candidate pursues the programme via correspondence. The duration of an MBA in distance/correspondence format is 2-5 years. An MBA from IGNOU is one of the most popular Distance MBA programmes in India.

Online MBA: With changing time, online MBA has become one of the most popular mode of MBA programme owing to ease and flexibility. There are no physical classes, thus a student can pursue from anywhere. The duration of the MBA in the online format is 1 to 4 years.

Executive MBA: One-year MBA or commonly known as Executive Â MBA is designed for working professionals who don't want to leave their jobs to pursue an MBA and have more than five years of work experience. The syllabus is similar to Full-Time MBA but it is of a shorter duration.

Q. What is an MBA?

Ans: MBA is a two- year post-graduate degree that provides theoretical and practical training for business, investment and entrepreneurship management. The total duration of a full time MBA degree programme is two years equally segregated into 4 equal semesters. The course structure includes key aspects such as organisational behaviour, finance management, marketing management, operations management, HR management, business communication, etc.

 

Q. What is the difference between MBA and PGDM?

Ans: Both the courses are equivalent to each other and has equal merit in terms of skills, learning and training imparted. Only difference is that after completing the MBA course, the student gets a degree while after completing PGDM, the student gets a diploma.

 

Q. What is the eligibility criteria for an MBA?

Ans: Candidates with a minimum 50 percent marks in any bachelorâ€™s degree from any background be it commerce, science, arts, can choose for taking a postgraduate degree in MBA. For reserved category student, the criteria is 45 percent.

 

Q. How can I get admission in an MBA course?

Ans: Admission process in an MBA program includes entrance exam, group discussion, personal interview and some other rounds. Many colleges offer admission on the basis of graduation score only. To get admission in MBA course, the candidate will have to fill an application form and follow the admission process.

 

Q. Do I need to appear for entrance exam for MBA admission?

Ans: Entrance exams are of national, state or university level. Most of the Tier-1 and Tier-2 MBA / PGDM institutes in India accept candidates on the basis of entrance exam score. So, if you want to pursue MBA from a reputed college, taking an entrance exam is a must.

 

Q. Which MBA course or specialisations is the best?

Ans: The most common courses or specialisations are Marketing, Finance, Human Resource (HR), Operations and many new career specific specialisations have cropped up over time. All of MBA courses or specialisations have bright prospects for students it the field of business management. However, the best MBA course depends solely on the candidates and their interest.

 

Q. What is the fee of MBA?

Ans: The fee of an MBA program depends upon college, specialisation but there are merit based scholarships also available. The MBA program fees in India can vary from Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 30 Lac.

Q. What is the career scope of MBA in India?

Ans: Since an MBA degree incorporates the theoretical concepts of various subjects with on-the-job training, the scope of MBA is not restricted to only a handful of industries. Some of the most popular and fascinating areas to work after obtaining a degree in this professional course are Retail and Corporate Sector, Banking and Financial Institutions, Government Agencies and Organizations, Media Sector, Information Technology , Hospitality Sector and Entrepreneurship.

 

Q. What is the salary of an MBA fresher?

Ans: The salary of an MBA graduate starts from 7 lakhs to 25 Lakhs per annum on average. There are various factors affecting the salary structure such as the university placement, academic score, job profile, skills, and many more.

 

Q. What are some of the popular job profiles for MBA candidates?

Ans: Job profile largely depends upon the specialisation and sector of the candidate. Some of the best job profiles for an MBA candidate are Sales Manager, Executive Manager, Investment Banker, Financial Manager, Product Manager, Marketing Manager, Logistics Manager, Business Analyst, Project Manager, Operations Manager, Human Resource Manager to name a few.
