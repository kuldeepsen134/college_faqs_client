About GMAT Examination

 

GMAC is a US-based Pearson VUE, an educational testing service. It is the sole conducting authority of GMAT all over the world, for MBA admissions. The GMAT score is accepted at more than 2300 colleges and 7000 programs around the globe.

GMAT exam is taken throughout the year numerous times, so the candidate can select a day most suitable for them. Since the GMAT is a computer adaptive test, candidates are required to prepare well and then take the test.

A candidate can appear 5 times a year and 8 times in a lifetime, in regular interval of time for the GMAT entrance exam but there should be a gap of at least 16 days between any two successive attempts. The scorecard will be sent to the desired university in 20 days after the result is declared. Candidates who are interested can find more information about the GMAT exam below.

GMAT Eligibility

There are no set criteria for GMAT eligibility by GMAC. However, one should always meet the eligibility criteria set by the university/college one aspires to get into after giving the GMAT. Some of the basic criteria for GMAT are:

·         The candidate must have completed 18 years of age

·         There is no upper age limit for the candidate

·         If the candidate is between 13 to 17 years old, they should have permission in writing from their parents or legal guardian

·         Candidates who wish to enroll in an MBA programme should possess a graduate degree in any discipline from a recognized university with 60% marks

 

 

GMAT Registrations

A candidate has the liberty of giving the GMAT exam anytime in the year as per their convenience. A candidate is only allowed five attempts in one year. The important thing to note is that the candidates can reschedule their next attempt only after 16 days of taking the first attempt. Steps to register for GMAT are:

·         Visit the official GMAT website

·         Candidate needs to create his/her account.

·         Give your personal details like Name, address, etc.

·         Provide your academic and work details.

·         Verify and submit your details.

·         Schedule your exam. Choose your closest or preferred test center.

·         Select the preferred date and time slot.

·         Pay the GMAT Registration fee online through debit or credit card.

·         After paying the fee your GMAT Registration is complete.

GMAT Exam Pattern

The GMAT exam is arranged by the GMAC. It is a computer adaptive test which means the difficulty level of the questions increases as the candidate gives correct answers. The syllabus is mainly divided into 4 sections topics: Analytical Writing, Integrated Reasoning, Quantitative Reasoning, and Verbal Reasoning.

GMAT Score Calculation

Each of your five GMAT Scores i.e. 4 GMAT sections and the total score is reported on a fixed scale. These are your official scores that appear on your GMAT Score Report. GMAT Total Score Report includes Verbal and Quantitative scores and does not include any other section into account. The GMAT Score Report comprises of these elements:

·         Your Quantitative Score (0 – 60), with percentile

·         Your Verbal Score (0 – 60), with percentile

·         Your Total GMAT Score (200 – 800), with percentile

·         AWA Score (half-integers from 0 to 6), with percentile

·         Integrated Reasoning score (integer from 1 to 8)

FAQs GMAT Exam 2023

 

Q. What is the GMAT full-form?

Ans. The GMAT full-form is the Graduate Management Admission Test which is a standised test that measures different abilities of candidates.

 

Q. Who is eligible to apply for GMAT 2022?

 

Ans. Candidates with a minimum of 60% aggregate in the Bachelor\'s degree can participate in GMAT. This is the eligibility criterion for the GMAT Exam and not for getting admission. The admission criteria will be different for each college/university. Candidates should separately check the admission criteria for the colleges they intend to apply.

 

Q. What are the common facts about GMAT?

Ans. Common facts about the GMAT Exam are:

·         Students are also not allowed to appear for their GMAT exam more than 5 times in a 12-month rolling period.

·         Candidates can attempt the GMAT exam a maximum of 8 times in their lifetime.

·         The unique aspect about GMAT is that it does not have a set exam date. Students have the flexibility to choose from the many available GMAT exam dates to appear for the exam.

 

Q. Is GMAT a tough exam?

Ans. GMAT is a tough exam and it requires the right strategy and hard work to score 700+.Its computer-adaptive nature, timing pressure, exam format are some reasons which make the exam hard.

 

Q. What is the difference between GMAT and GRE?

Ans. The GMAT is the standardized test for getting admission to a business school whereas GRE is required for getting into a graduate school. Now, even certain business schools have also started accepting GRE as well.

 

Q. What is the difference between GMAT vs CAT?

Ans. GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test) and  CAT (Common Admission Test) are the two most widely accepted management entrance tests, scores of which are accepted by all the top B-schools, including the IIMs. The basic difference, between the two, is that while CAT is a national-level entrance exam for students looking for admission to Top Business Schools in India, whereas GMAT is held at a global platform and helps students for admission to top B-Schools worldwide. Conducted by Graduate Management Admission Council, GMAT scores are acceptable mainly in the case of NRI/Foreign students who want to study in India.

 

Q. What is a computer adaptive test?

Ans. GMAT is a computer-adaptive test, it adapts to the performance of the candidate while he is giving the test. The difficulty level of the questions changes based on whether the candidate is answering the questions right or wrong. If the candidate is answering the questions right, the level of the questions will rise, similarly, if the candidate is answering the questions wrong, the candidate will start getting easy questions. Also, as the next question is generated based on the performance in the current question, the candidate cannot visit the previous question. The candidate needs to answer a question whether he is able to solve it or not in order to move ahead in the exam.

 

Q. What are the documents one has to carry to the GMAT test center?

Ans. Candidates will have to carry a valid passport and the appointment confirmation letter received at the time of registration to the test center.

 

Q. What is Percentile vs Marks assessment in GMAT?

Ans. The GMAT score includes percentile that compares your performance with other test-takers. Scoring high percentile means you performed better than most of the candidates who appeared for the exam. Similarly, scoring low percentile signifies that you scored lower than most of the candidates.

Q. How can I Reschedule and Retake GMAT Exam?

Ans. Rescheduling of the GMAT exam can be done through two major modes: online and via phone.

·         1 to 14 days prior to appointment: US $150

·         15 to 60 days prior to appointment: US $100

·         More than 60 days prior to appointment: US $50

Please note that the exam cannot be rescheduled on the test date. The only difference in rescheduling the exam is through the phone is that you would need to pay a $10 surcharge along with the rescheduling fee. Retaking the GMAT exam can be done after 16 days from your previous attempt.

 

Q. What is the Executive Assessment Exam?

Ans. For Executive MBA, GMAC has introduced a new Mini GMAT called Executive Assessment exam. This exam is specially designed for EMBA applicants and is of considerably shorter duration. It is meant to test the analytical and logical thinking of the applicant, which is more suited for the Executive MBA course.

 

Q. Are candidates able to preview their scores?

Ans. Candidates will not be able to preview their exam scores at the end of the exam, however, they will be able to view their scores before selecting programs that they want to send scores to.

 

Q. How to send my GMAT scores to B-schools?

Ans. GMAT scores can be sent in two ways to B-schools. While registering for the GMAT exam, candidates have the option of sending the score report to five programs for free. Also, if candidates are not decided yet and need more time to send their scores, they can always request sending an additional score report by paying $35.

 

Q. What if I want to score report of the GMAT exam taken over five years ago?

Ans. GMAT score is valid for a period of 5 years. However, it is advisable to check with the college before going to request an old score report, a majority of the colleges do not accept the scores of the exam taken over 5 years ago.
