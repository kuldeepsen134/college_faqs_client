About MAT Examination

 

Common Management Aptitude Test or MAT is a national-level entrance exam conducted by the All India Management Association (AIMA) for admissions to MBA/PGDM courses. The exam is conducted four times in a year – February, May, September and December. Qualifying candidates are eligible for admissions to 800+ B-schools accepting MAT scores.

MAT Eligibility Criteria

As per AIMA, minimum Eligibility Criteria for students who wish to appear for MAT 2022 are as follows:

·         Candidate must be an Indian resident.

·         No Upper Age Limit is set by AIMA to take MAT exam.

·         Candidates must have completed graduation in any discipline from a recognized University.

·         Candidates in their final year of UG programs like B.A., B.Sc, B.Com, B.Tech, etc. can also apply.

·         AIMA does not prescribe a minimum percentage of marks in graduation for appearing in MAT exam. However, for admission into colleges, candidates will be required to obtain a minimum percentage that varies from college to college.

·         No Minimum work experience is essential for taking MAT 2022 exam.

·         No limit on the number of attempts.

 

MAT Registration Process

MAT application form is online and is same for all the three exam modes - PBT, CBT and IBT. Candidates can fill the MAT application form 2022 of preferred exam phase on AIMA MAT exam website by paying exam fees of INR 1,850. Check below the process to apply for MAT::

·         Open AIMA-MAT website in a new browser

·         Click on “Fresh Candidate to create Log In” tab: https://mat.aima.in/

·         Enter name, date of birth, email ID, mobile phone number and a password

·         Fill the MAT exam application form with relevant information

·         Select preferred test mode (CBT, PBT, IBT), test cities and five B-Schools

·         • Upload a recent passport size photo and scanned signature

·         Make fee payment via debit/credit card, net banking or e-wallet to complete the process

MAT Exam Preparation Tips

A candidate should devote at least six months to prepare for MAT exam. For a fruitful preparation, they should focus on improving their problem-solving skills with the help of previous years' MAT question papers and online mock test, etc. The MAT preparation strategy should be well planned and in accordance with the MAT exam pattern and MAT syllabus. Some of the tips for preparation are::

  • Read best books for MAT exam preparation, magazines, newspapers and online blogs on a daily basis to improve reading skills, understanding skills as well as for keeping track of current affairs of national and international level
  • Practice MAT question papers, sample papers and online mock tests to improve speed and accuracy as well as to develop familiarity with the test pattern
  • Regularly analyse your preparation level to know your strengths and weakness in each section of the MAT syllabus
  • Target topics carrying more weightage in each section of MAT to score well in the exam
  • Time management is the key to attempt maximum questions in MAT so, don’t waste time on tricky or tough questions

 

MAT Admit Card

MAT admit card is released online. The MAT admit cards can be download from the official website. In order to download the hall ticket, candidates need to key in the registration number and date of birth on the login page of AIMA-MAT website. Given below are the steps to download MAT admit card::

  • Open the MAT 2022 website
  • Go to 'download/view'
  • Select ' MAT admit card' from the drop down
  • Enter email ID, password and date of birth
  • Download the MAT admit card

 

MAT Exam Day Guidelines

  • For MAT IBT mode, candidates should arrange a laptop/desktop and check internet connection at least one hour before the exam timing.
  • For MAT IBT mode, candidates should wear formal clothes and keep the admit card and other documents ready for remote verification process.
  • MAT PBT and CBT are conducted from 10:00 AM to 12:30 PM. Candidates have to report to the exam centre by 8:30 AM.
  • Candidates should carry a blue or black ball point pen if they are taking the exam in PBT mode.
  • Phone, calculator, electronic watch or any such item is prohibited at the exam centre hence, candidates must ensure they don't carry any such item on exam day.
  • Digital or photocopy of the MAT admit card will not be considered a valid ID proof so carry the original copy of your admit card.

Also carry below-mentioned documents to the MAT exam centre for PBT and CBT mode exams::

  • Admit card: Two printouts
  • Self-declaration for COVID-19: Attached with admit card
  • Identity proof: Any of the following documents (in original) — Voter ID, Aadhaar Card, Election I-Card, Passport, Driving License and PAN Card
  • Proof of name change (if any): In case, you have changed your name, you will be required to carry a document supporting the change of name

FAQs MAT Exam 2023

 

Q: What is MAT?

Ans: MAT is one of the important national level MBA entrance exams for admission to MBA / PGDM in 1000+ AICTE approved management institutes. MAT 2023 will be conducted by National Testing Agency (NTA) as computer based test of three hours duration.

 

Q. When is MAT 2023?

Ans: MAT is held once a year and MAT 2023 is expected to be held in the month of March or April 2023. The exam is conducted at various exam centres across 124 cities in India. You can choose the test city according to your preference.

 

Q: Is MAT conducted online or offline?

Ans: MAT exam is a computer based test mode conducted online at designated examination centres only. Candidates are required to punch in their answers with the help of keypad and mouse of the computer system provided.

 

Q: What are all the sections available in the MAT 2023 syllabus?

Ans: There are five sections available in the MAT exam syllabus, namely:

1.       Quantitative Techniques & Data Interpretation

2.       Logical Reasoning

3.       Language Comprehension & Verbal Ability

4.       General Awareness

5.       Innovation and Entrepreneurship

 

Q: Can I go back and forth between the sections during MAT exam?

Ans: Yes, you can because there will be no sectional wise time limit.

 

 Q: When is the last date to apply for MAT 2023?

Ans: The MAT registration 2023 schedule is not yet announced. NTA conducts MAT exam and schedule of MAT 2023 will be announced in January or February 2023. The application form filling window will be live for about one month for candidates to register and pay the fees for the exam.

 

Q: What are the eligibility criteria to apply for MAT?

Ans: Any candidate who has secured passing marks in graduation can apply for MAT. Candidates must ensure that they have cleared graduation. Candidates in the final year of their bachelor's degree are also eligible to apply for MAT exam provided their result will be declared before admission for academic year begins.

 

Q: Is there any age restriction for appearing for MAT exam?

Ans: No, there are no age restrictions for the MAT exam 2023.

 

Q: For how long is MAT score valid for admission?

Ans: Similar to other MBA entrance exams, MAT score is also valid for one year.

 

Q. How many colleges accept MAT score for admission?

Ans: Over 1,000+ MBA / PGDM colleges across India accept MAT score for admission. These includes some of the top colleges like JBIMS - Mumbai, Goa Institute of Management - Goa, KJ Somaiya Institute of Management - Mumbai, SIMSREE - Mumbai, Great Lakes Institute of Management - Chennai, PUMBA - Pune, etc.

 

Q: What are the reservation criteria for MAT?

Ans: There is no reservation criterion for MAT exam. However, MAT participating institutes have to abide by reservation criteria for educational institutions set by the Government of India during admission process.

 

Q: What is the MAT application fee?

 

Ans: MAT application fee is Rs. 2,000 for general category male student. For general category female/reserved category students the application fee is Rs. 1,000.

 

Q: Is there a provision of rechecking or revaluating MAT answer sheets?

Ans: No, there is no provision of rechecking or revaluating MAT answer sheets. Marks allotted to candidates in the scorecard will be final. However, once the Answer Key is released, a student has the chance to raise a claim by following the due process and if claim is found to be correct than it is considered.

 

Q: Till when will MAT scorecard is available for download on the NTA website?

Ans: The MAT result is available for 60 days from the result date on the MAT-NTA website. After two months, they will have to put special requisition with the exam authorities for MAT scorecard.

 

Q: Will the NTA release MAT 2023 merit list after result declaration?

Ans: Yes, the NTA is expected to release MAT 2023 merit list along with the result. The merit list will be released in the form of PDF, containing each test taker's section-wise scores, overall score and overall percentile.

 

 

Q: Is there any difference between MAT score and percentile?

Ans: Starting from MAT 2022, NTA has stopped giving percentile. Students are only issued with score and rank.

 

Q: How many times can I attempt MAT?

Ans: Since MAT is an annual exam, you can attempt it only once in the particular year. However, there is no limit on number of times you can attempt for MAT.

 

Q: How many candidates appear for MAT?

Ans: Around 50,000 to 60,000 student appear for MAT every year.

 

Q: Is there will be negative marking in the MAT 2023 exam?

Ans: Yes, one mark will get reduced for each incorrect answer. For every correct answer a student will get 4 marks. In total the MAT exam is of 400 marks with 100 questions equally divided into five sections.

 

Q: How can I request to change the exam centre?

Ans: Once MAT admit card is issued, no request for change in exam centre will be entertained.

 

Q: Can I carry a watch to the MAT exam centre?

Ans: No, candidates are not allowed to carry few items such as a wristwatch, mobile, calculator, etc to the MAT test centre.

 

Q. Can I change preferred test centre after submitting a form?

Ans: No, once the application form has been submitted you cannot change the test centre. However, test centre is allotted as per the preference given by the student. However, if NTA announces application correction date, then you may be allowed to change your MAT test centre.

 

Q. Do government MBA colleges in India accept MAT score?

Ans: Yes, many government colleges like PUMBA - Pune, Indian Institute of Information Technology - Allahabad, NIT - Rourkela etc.
