About ATMA Examination

 

AIMS Test for Management Admissions or ATMA is a national-level exam conducted by the Association of Indian Management Schools (AIMS) for admission to MBA, PGDM, MCA and PGDBA courses. More than 750 management institutes across India accept ATMA scores for admission to their management programmes. ATMA question paper comprises six sections with 30 questions in each section.

ATMA Exam is conducted multiple times a year, across 5 sessions – February Session, April Session, May Session, July Session, and August Session. Questions in the exam are asked from Analytical Reasoning, Quantitative and Verbal Skills. ATMA is a moderate difficulty level exam leading to admission in top B-schools like Welingkar, VIT, XIMR, SDMIMD, JIMS, Jaipuria among others.

ATMA 2023 Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility criteria of ATMA exam are similar to other management entrance exams. Check out the ATMA eligibility criteria below:

·         Candidate must be an Indian resident.

·         Any graduate with a minimum of 50 per cent aggregate or equivalent CGPA, from a recognised university, is eligible to apply.

·         Candidates who are in their final year of graduation can apply provisionally.

·         There is no upper age limit for applying and appearing in ATMA exam.

 

ATMA Application Process

After the notification of ATMA February Exam, the application form will be available online on the official website https://www.atmaaims.com. In order to avoid any last-minute delays, candidates must stay aware of the start and last date of ATMA registration. The ATMA application process includes the following steps:

1.       Creating a new user profile by filling out the ATMA registration form with details such as name, date of birth, gender, email ID and phone number, etc.

2.       Paying the ATMA application fee via credit/debit card, internet banking, UPI or other banking wallets.

a.       Candidates need to make payment of Rs. 1,600 for the ATMA 2023 entrance exam. The dates of fee payment will be notified.

3.       Filling out the complete ATMA application form using PID number and password.

a.       After making payments, use the login credentials and enter PID created along with the password to log in. Now start filling out the ATMA application form.

b.       Fill in details such as name, email id, gender, address, nationality, educational background, qualification details, and a professional degree (if any). While filling out the application form, choose the test centre of your choice.

4.       Uploading the required documents in the prescribed format.

a.       Candidates need to upload documents such as a photograph, signature, OBC(NC)/SC/ST/DAP certificate, mark sheets, and other documents for the purpose of verification.

5.       Once all the above steps are done, download the confirmation page and keep safe its printout for reference.

ATMA Exam Syllabus

The three major sections in the ATMA syllabus are Quantitative Skills, Verbal Skills, and Analytical Reasoning Skills. Each section is further divided into two parts. Each of the six sections will have 30 questions in MCQ format. Various topics covered in these sections are:

 

Syllabus

Important Topics

Quantitative Skills

Percentage, Time and Work, Pie Chart and Bar Graph, Profit and Loss, Number System, Mensuration, Time-Speed-Distance, Data Interpretation, Basic Arithmetic, Simple and Compound Interest, Algebra, Probability, Average, Geometry, Progression, Inequalities, Allegation, Ratio and Proportion Variations, Set Theory, Quadratic Equation, Functions, Logarithm, Series.

Verbal Skills

Reading Comprehension, Synonyms & Antonyms, Grammar, Jumbled Sentences, Word Meaning, Sentence Completion.

Analytical Reasoning Skills

Strike the odd one out, Blood Relations, Analogy, An arrangement of letters, Visual Reasoning, Syllogism, Coding-Decoding, Verbal Logic, Data Sufficiency, Statement– Conclusion, Number Series

 

 

ATMA Preparation Tips

Candidates planning to appear for February exam of ATMA 2023 must start there preparation six to nine months before the entrance exam. They must devise an exam preparation plan which should include section wise preparation schedule:

Quantitative Ability and Data Analysis:

·         Revise all the sections of mathematics by solving questions from class 10 and class 12 math textbooks.

·         Learn all the common formulae and learn tricks to solve questions quickly.

·         Solve 20 – 25 questions daily of all types to increase speed, accuracy and consistency.

 

Verbal Ability or Language Comprehension:

·         Improve your vocabulary.

·         Regularly practice questions of sentence jumble, paragraph jumble. Practice reading paragraphs and solving questions on them. Regularly read magazines and newspapers to learn word meanings, idioms, and phrases.

·         Refer to the grammar books to improve your knowledge of basic grammar.

Analytical Reasoning:

·         This section of ATMA is considered to be one of the most difficult ones. To do well in this section, it is recommended that you regularly practice solving puzzles and riddles.

·         Practice questions on coding and decoding to improve your critical thinking. Practice reading statements and try to arrive at logical conclusions and identify fallacious logic.

·         Practice as much of previous years’ question papers to get an idea about the question pattern.

ATMA 2023 Exam Day Guidelines

Candidates appearing for the ATMA exam must keep in mind the following points for the exam day:

·         ATMA exam timing is 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM, so the candidates need to reach the exam venue by 1:00 PM to complete identification and other processes.

·         The candidates need to carry two printouts of the ATMA admit card with passport size photograph affixed to it and photo ID proof (original and photocopy) issued by the Government of India.

·         Candidates also need to carry a printout of the ATMA application form.

·         Candidates should affix the photograph which matches the application form.

·         Gadgets such as digital watches, statistical tables, calculators, etc. are not permitted in the exam centre.

·         AIMS will release counselling date on its official website after the result is announced. The Counselling process enables the candidates to understand their options for opting for the college or university accordingly.

FAQs – ATMA Exam 2023

 

 

Q: What is ATMA?

Ans: ATMA (AIMS Test for Management Admissions) is a national-level entrance exam conducted by AIMS (Association of Indian Management School). It is a single-window test held for admission to various post-graduate programs like MBA, PGDM, MMS, MCA etc.

 

Q. When is ATMA 2023 February exam?

Ans: ATMA 2023 February exam is expected to be held in the 4th week of February, 2023. Final dates would e announced once the notification comes out on the official website of ATMA.

 

 

Q: When is the last date to apply for ATMA 2023?

Ans: The ATMA registration 2023 schedule is not yet announced. However, it is expected the that registration will start from 2nd week of December, 2022 and will end in 3rd Week of February, 2022.

 

Q: Can I appear for ATMA exam more than once?

 

Ans: Yes, ATMA is held four to five times a year. Candidates can appear more than once and the score of the last exam attempted would be considered for admissions.

 

 

Q. What is the application fee for ATMA?

 

Ans: The application fee for ATMA is INR 1,600 including GST. There is a 25% fee concession for candidates belonging to NE states and women candidates.

 

 

Q: What are all the sections available in the ATMA syllabus?

Ans: There are three sections available in the ATMA exam syllabus, namely:

1.       Analytical Reasoning Skills

2.       Verbal Skills

3.       Quantitative Skills

 

Q: What is PID number?

 

Ans: The PID number or Personal Identification Number is a unique number assigned to the candidate at the time of filling the ATMA application form which can be used by him/her to log in to their ATMA account.

 

 

Q. What is the eligibility criterion for the ATMA 2023 exam?

 

Ans: Any graduate candidate with a 50 per cent aggregate (45 per cent for reserved category) can apply for the exam. The candidates must also be a citizen of India.

 

 

Q: Is there any age restriction for the candidates who appear for the ATMA exam?

 

Ans: No, there is no age restriction for the candidates who apply for the ATMA exam, however, they must fulfil the other eligibility criteria before appearing for the ATMA exam.

 

 

Q: Can I pay the ATMA registration fee by cash/DD or net banking?

 

Ans: ATMA registration fee can be paid only through debit/credit cards. There is no provision of paying the application fee offline.

 

 

Q. I am unable to upload my signatures in the registration form. What should I do?

 

Ans: There is a set limit to upload a photograph and digital signatures in the ATMA registration form. The size of the file should be between 20 KB to 50 KB. The dimensions of the signatures should be 140 X 60 Pixels.

 

 

Q. How many test cities can I choose while filling out the ATMA application form?

 

Ans: Candidates can choose up to two test cities according to their preferences at the time of filling the application form.

 

 

Q. Is there any reservation in ATMA exam result?

 

Ans: No, the ATMA application form is the same for all the candidates. They have to choose the category they belong to and provide the supporting documents at the time of admission to the respective college. Reservation is totally college dependent.

 

 

Q. Which documents are needed for uploading while filling the ATMA registration form?

 

Ans: Candidates need to upload their passport size photograph, digitally scanned signatures, caste certificate (if applicable), copies of Class 10, Class 12 and graduation mark sheets. Sponsored applicants are required to upload sponsoring certificates as per the format available on the ATMA website.

 

 

Q. Can I make corrections in the ATMA application form after submission?

 

Ans: No changes can be made once the ATMA application form has been submitted. Candidates must fill the application form carefully and check the details before submitting the form.

 

 

Q. Is there any negative marking in ATMA?

 

Ans: Candidates will be awarded one mark for every correct answer and 0.25 marks are deducted for every incorrect answer.

 

 

Q. Can I change my test centre after the admit card has been issued?

 

Ans: No, candidates cannot change their test centres once the admit card has been issued. The test centre can only be changed if the request has been made at least 10 days prior to the exam and is subject to the availability of seats and approval of test administrator.

 

 

Q. How many colleges accept ATMA score for admission?

Ans: Over 700+ MBA / PGDM colleges across India accept ATMA score for admission. These includes some of the top colleges like Prin L.N. Welingkar Institute of Management Development and Research (WeSchool) - Mumbai, Jaipuria Institute of Management - Lucknow, ISBM Pune: International School of Business & Media, Pune Institute of Business Management - Pune, IPE Hyderabad: Institute of Public Enterprise etc.
