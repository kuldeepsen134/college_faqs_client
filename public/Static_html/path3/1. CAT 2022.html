About CAT Examination

 

CAT stands for Common Admission Test. It is a national-level post graduate management course entrance examination for MBA / PGDM. The test is conducted by any of the top 6 Indian Institute Management (IIM) every year for admission in 20 IIMs and 2000+ other CAT score accepting B-schools. Every year around 2,00,000 candidates apply for CAT exam to get admission to 5000+ MBA / PGP seats of IIM’s.

Apart from IIM’s, CAT is also a mandatory entrance exam for MBA admission in colleges like FMS Delhi, SPJIMR Mumbai, MDI Gurgaon, IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Kharagpur; NITIE Mumbai and hundreds of other top B-schools in India. Salient features of CAT exam are:

·         It is a computer based test

·         It is conducted at more than 150 cities with approximately 400+ test venues

·         CAT exam duration is reduced to two hours from earlier three hours since 2020

·         Subject wise the exam is divided into three sections:

o   Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC)

o   Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR)

o   Quantitative Ability (QA)

·         Each section is to be attempted in a fixed individual time of 40 minutes

·         Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) will offer admission in 2023 on the basis of CAT 2022 score only

According to 2021 official website of CAT, around 2.30 lakhs eligible candidates registered for the CAT entrance test. The overall CAT test-takers attendance was approximately 83% at about 1.92 Lac candidates. Out of total appeared candidates 65% were males and 35% were females, and 2 candidates represent transgender community.

CAT Eligibility Criteria 2022

The minimum eligibility criterion for CAT 2022 exam is graduation in any stream. Detailed CAT eligibility criteria are listed below:

·         Candidates must hold a bachelor's degree with minimum 50 per cent aggregate or equivalent CGPA (45 per cent aggregate for SC, ST and PwD category candidates) from a recognised university to appear in CAT 2022.

·         Candidates appearing for the final year of bachelor's degree / equivalent qualification or awaiting the result are also eligible to apply for CAT 2022 exam.

·         Candidates who have completed a professional degree (CA / CS / ICWA / FCAI) with the required percentage can also appear for CAT.

·         There is no age limit for appearing in CAT 2022.

·         Work experience is not mandatory for appearing in CAT 2022.

CAT Exam Syllabus and Pattern

Unlike other management exams where GK is also part of syllabus, the syllabus of CAT exam has remained the same for more than last ten years. The syllabus is still focused on Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR) and Quantitative Aptitude (QA) with equal importance to each section. The in-focus topics of CAT 2022 syllabus for exam preparation are:

Syllabus

Topics

Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC)

Long & Short Passages, Jumbled Paragraphs; Para-summary, Picking the Out of context sentence from a jumbled paragraph

Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR)

Data Charts, Data Tables, Bar Graphs, Venn Diagrams, Seating arrangement, Team formation, Clocks & Calendars

Quantitative Aptitude (QA)

Arithmetic, Modern Math, Number System, Algebra, Mensuration, Trigonometry, Geometry

 

The current CAT exam pattern, exam duration, no of questions is as follows:

·         Exam Duration is 120 Minutes: CAT 2022 is a computer delivered test with total time duration of 120 minutes.

·         Section wise Time Duration: Total time of 120 minutes is divided into 3 sections of 40 minutes each for VARC, DILR and QA.

·         66 Questions: Like CAT 2021, CAT 2022 exam will have 66 questions divided in 3 sections of VARC, DILR and Quantitative Ability.

·         MCQ and Non-MCQ type of questions – In CAT 2022, approximately 48 – 50 questions would be MCQ and 18 – 20 questions are expected to be non-MCQ.

·         Marking Scheme – MCQ carry 3 marks for every correct response with Negative marking of 1/3 (-1) for every wrong MCQ response. No negative marking for wrong non-MCQ response.

·         CAT cut off Requirement by IIMs: A minimum score between 70 to 90 percentiles in CAT 2022 would be needed in each of the 3 sections, for candidates to qualify for final admission round.

 

 

 

CAT 2022 Exam Preparation Tips

CAT is one of the most competitive entrance exams with more than 2 lac candidates competing for around 5000 seats of IIMs. Hence, it becomes essential to prepare well for the exam. To begin with CAT 2022 preparation, one should solve & analyze previous year question papers, go through the detailed subject-wise exam analysis and focus on covering at least 70% of the syllabus in 2-3 months. Some of the most important tip and tricks of CAT exam preparation include:

·         Identify your strengths and weaknesses and make a list of topics as per it.

·         Join online study groups on platforms such as Facebook, YouTube, Telegram, etc.

·         Collect relevant study material, test sets and recommended books for CAT preparation.

·         Make day-wise plan as per topics and syllabus by allotting time to VARC, DILR and QA preparation.

·         Practice two-three questions from the topic studied on the same day to assess how much you have learnt.

·         Start from topics wise test series to be followed by subject wise and then overall.

·         Increase the speed gradually to cover maximum number of topics each day.

CAT Preparation for Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning (DILR)

Questions in Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning (DILR) section are asked in the form of Sets of 4-5 questions. Cracking one set accurately can fetch you 12-15 marks. Important tips for preparation of DILR are:

·         For DI preparation, solve at least 5-6 sets every week from different types of data sets- bar graphs, line graphs, pie charts, tabular data, and caselets.

·         To manage time while solving DI questions, be thorough with important ratio-percentage conversions, profit & loss, averages, and other arithmetic topics.

·         For LR preparation, pick one LR topic for a day and solve 20-30 questions of varying difficulty.

·         Arun Sharma CAT Books can be referred to prepare for DILR and practice 500+ questions.

·         Join online study groups on Facebook, Telegram, etc. and join CAT mock test series provided by coaching institutes.

CAT Preparation for Quantitative Aptitude (QA)

Arithmetic accounts for around 50% of the questions asked in CAT Quant section and the least number of questions are asked from Numbers and Modern Mathematics. A student should focus more on preparing for Arithmetic, Algebra, and Geometry.

·         Be updated about the basic calculation concepts- ratio-percentage conversion, squares and cubes till 30, square roots & cube roots, averages, exponential theorems, divisibility rules, concept of numbers, units digit, alligation, probability, P&C, and tables till 25.

·         For Arithmetic preparation, focus more on topics like Profit & Loss, TSD, Time & Work, Mixtures, Averages, and Percentages.

·         In Geometry, the most important topics include Triangles, Circles, and Parallelograms along with important theorems and formulas on triangles and circles. Question can also be expected from Mensuration- both 2D and 3D.

·         Pick 2 topics every day and solve 15 questions on each topic daily.

·         Join online study groups on Facebook, Telegram, etc. and join CAT mock test series provided by coaching institutes.

·         Arun Sharma CAT Books can be referred to prepare for Quant and practice 500+ questions.

·         Attempt sectional mock tests in every 2 – 3 days with the time limit of 40 minutes. Review your performance after every mock test and work on your errors.

CAT Preparation for Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension (VARC)

Reading Comprehension (RC) accounts for more than 50% of the questions asked in Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension (VARC). The important topics in Verbal Ability include para jumbles, odd one out, para summary, and error detection.

·         To increase your vocabulary, read newspapers, magazines, or online articles on a daily basis. You can also refer to the book- Word Power Made Easy by Norman Lewis.

·         Arun Sharma CAT Books can be referred to prepare for VARC and practice 500+ questions.

·         To increase reading speed for RC passages, start with short passages (250-350 words) and then gradually move to 500+ word passages.

·         Solve CAT previous year question papers, sample papers, and various online mock test series to get hold on a variety of questions.

CAT 2022 Result & IIMs’ Final Selection Process

CAT 2022 exam official result declaration date is expected in second week of January 2023; however, IIMs may release it in the first week of January 2023. IIMs will start announcing their shortlist candidates on the basis of CAT scores and other parameters in their shortlisting criteria for final selection round.

The admission process in IIMs depends upon performance in three major areas:

1.       CAT 2022 exam score

2.       Academic record

3.       Performance in Personal Interview rounds (GD-WAT-PI)

The final admission offer will be made to the candidates in the months of April/May 2023 by IIMs 

The final admission offer depends upon performance in all the above three stages of IIMs selection process. While the older IIMs conduct their individual selection process, 9 new and baby IIMs conduct common admission process (CAP) for admission. Remaining IIMs conduct their individual selection process. 

Top IIMs Conduct Individual Admission Process

IIM Ahmedabad, IIM Bangalore, IIM Calcutta, IIM Lucknow, IIM Indore, IIM Kozhikode and IIM Shillong individually shortlist the candidates and conduct their own admission process by awarding weightage to academics, diversity, work experience apart from CAT exam score

Common Admission Process (CAP) for 9 IIMs

The final selection process at 9 new IIMs is conducted as a common process. One of the six new IIMs co-ordinates this selection process which is referred to as Common Admission Process (CAP). The CAP 2023 will consist of common WAT and PI round for 9 new IIMs namely IIM Kashipur, IIM Raipur, IIM Ranchi,  IIM Trichy, IIM Udaipur and some of the newest IIMs like IIM Bodhgaya, IIM Sambalpur, IIM Sirmaura and IIM Jammu.

IIM Nagpur, IIM Rohtak, IIM Visakhapatnam to conduct individual admission process

IIM Nagpur, IIM Rohtak and IIM Visakhapatnam do not participate in CAP round and conduct their own final selection process to make admission offer to the students. The final admission offers is generated as per the merit lists prepared on the basis of CAT scores, Academic profile, Work experience and gender diversity and weightage given to each category.

Q: Is there an age limit to appear in CAT exam?

Ans: No, there is no age limit to appear in the CAT exam. Any candidate who is a graduate or is in the final year of graduation is eligible to apply for CAT. Working professionals are also eligible to appear in CAT.

 

Q. I am in the second year of graduation; can I appear for CAT for practice purpose?

Ans: It is not advisable to appear for CAT with fulfilling the minimum eligibility criteria. If you haven't completed graduation and/or are not in the final year of bachelor's degree, you are not eligible.

 

Q. How is the CAT exam center allotted?

Ans: The examination conducting body allots the test centre on the basis of availability and preference made by the candidates on their application form. Although not guaranteed, candidates usually get the first or second choice CAT exam centre of their choice.

 

Q: Can I select my preference for shift and test date?

Ans: The CAT exam is conducted on one day in three shifts, so there is no choice of dates. The exam slot is allotted by the exam authority as per availability of seats at a particular exam centre. A candidate cannot choose exam shift / slot of their choice.

 

Q: Can I appear for more than one slot of CAT exam?

Ans: No, a candidate can appear for only one slot in the CAT exam. Even the test slot is allotted to the candidate by the exam authority and is not of candidates choosing. To ensure every candidate attempts the exam in only the slot allotted - admit cards, photo IDs, candidate photo and biometric records are verified at the exam centre.

 

Q: Are there any special provisions for PwD candidates?

Ans: Yes, there are special provisions for Persons with Disability (PwD) candidates, such as scribe assistance and extra exam hours. The PwD candidates get 20 minutes extra per CAT exam section. To avail these facilities, the candidates have to upload PwD and scribe certificates along with the CAT application form and carry the same to the exam centre.

 

Q: Am a EWS category student, which documents do I need to upload with CAT application form?

Ans: A EWS category student needs to upload the EWS category form available on the CAT website along with the CAT application form. The form should be duly filled and self-attested before submission.

 

Q: What is the marking scheme for CAT exam? Is there negative marking?

Ans: In the CAT exam MCQ carry 3 marks for every correct response with negative marking of 1/3 (-1) for every wrong MCQ response. No negative marking for wrong non-MCQ response. There is no negative marking for questions not attempted, so the candidates must choose their options wisely.

 

Q. How do we calculate the CAT percentile?

Ans: Percentile in any entrance exam is just a representation of the number of people who are behind you in the exam. The general formula to calculate percentile along with an examples is detailed out below:

Let us assume that 50 candidates appeared for the CAT exam. A candidate has scored 5th rank in the exam, which means 45 students scored less than you and are below you in rank. So the percentile would be calculated as: 45 / 50 x 100 = 90 Percentile.
