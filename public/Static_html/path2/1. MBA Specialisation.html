A business or organization has multiple, complex functions and departments. Each department, be it Sales, Marketing, HR, Finance, IT, Operations etc, all need to work together for achieving organization’s goals and objectives. MBA and PGDM program teaches all these department functions through its compulsory core course in first year, because all MBAs should have the basic understanding of how the business works. Having acquired this basic understanding of key functions, MBAs select one or two areas of specialization based on their interest and job market.

Interesting Facts about Specializations

  • There are over 30 MBA and PGDM specialisations being offered by management institutes
  • Traditionally, Finance, Marketing, International Management, Human Resources, IT/Systems, Operations Management have been the most sought-after MBA specialisations.
  • Overtime, with changing business needs and technology, B-schools have evolved and offering new specialisations such as Business Analytics, Digital Marketing, Global MBA, etc.
  • There are some niche specializations which many a times is linked to graduation degree also such as Agri-business, Health Care Management, Communications Management, Hospitality and Tourism, are also gaining immense popularity.

How to Choose an MBA Specialisation?

MBA and PGDM aspirants can choose to pursue General course or take up a specialisation of their choice in the said programme. But it is very important to choose the right specialization which can be done by taking into consideration the following parameters

Ø  Career scope of the specialization / placements/ internship opportunities

Ø  Long term career goal

Ø  Pedagogy of the specialisation of your choice

Ø  Reputation of the institute faculty in the specialisation of your choice

Ø  Course fee and Return on Investment (ROI) of the programme

FAQs on MBA and PGDM Specialisations

Q: Why should I opt for a specialised MBA / PGDM?

Ans: Specialised MBA / PGDM programmes teach the students detailed insights about the particular subject.  Not only do it teaches the theoretical aspects, but also provides practical and experiential learning as well. A student is prepared in such a way that s/he can be well accustomed with the managerial experiences as well.

 

Q: Do specialized / selected courses for MBA start from the first year?

Ans: In the General MBA, the teaching of selected subjects begins in the second or final year of a two-year full-time program.

 

Q: Is specialised MBA expensive than General MBA?

Ans: Fee structure of an MBA / PGDM programmes is more dependent on the institute than the specialization. Most of the colleges don’t have any difference in fees due to specializations. In general the traditional specializations like Finance, Marketing, HR etc are more expensive.

 

Q: How many MBA specialisations are there?

Ans: There are 30 plus MBA and PGDM specialisations offered across the B-schools of India. Given the advancement and innovation in every field, new niche specialisations have been added and more are expected to come up in the coming years.

 

Q: Which are the most popular MBA / PGDM specialisations?

Ans: Mmost popular and sought-after MBA /  PGDM specialisations are the tradional specialisations  such as Finance, Marketing, Human Resource Management, Operations and Supply Chain along with new specializations like International Business, Digital Marketing, Busines Analytics, etc.

 

Q: What are some of the emerging MBA specialisations?

Ans: There are several niche specialisations coming up. Some of the lesser-known MBA and PGDM specialisations include Sports Management, Luxury Brand Management, Corporate Social Responsibility, NGO Management, Rural Management, Hospitality Management to name a few.

Q: Is there any disadvantage of pursuing a specialised MBA?

Ans: Niche specialised MBA may restrict career growth or opportunity to explore other industry sectors, otherwise there is no disadvantage of pursuing a specialised MBA. A candidate must be aware that if the specialisation is not much popular, or the industry is too niche, then it will be difficult to establish a career in a short span of time in that area.

 

Q: What is the duration of specialized MBA programmes?

Ans: The duration of the full-time specialised MBA / PGDM programmes is same as a MBA General, i.e. two years. The duration will be longer than two years if the MBA programme is part-time or distance program.

 

Q: What are the factors to keep in mind for choosing the right MBA specialisation?

Ans: To choose a specialised MBA programme, the aspirants must keep in mind factors such as the scope of the specialisation, industry demand or career opportunity, to name a few. Apart from that, the B-School should be chosen on the basis of the pedagogy, infrastructure, placement records and faculty strength of the institute.

 

Q: What is a dual MBA specialisation?

Ans: MBA dual specialisation refers to specialisation in two different areas. It has to be ensured that the two specialisations complement each other and would shape up their career so that those do not cause any conflict in the career.

 

Q: Is a Dual MBA specialisation better than regular MBA specialisation?

Ans: Dual MBA specialisation is beneficial in a way that it helps gain expertise in two fields instead of one., such as Marketing and Finance. Dual MBA specialisation, if pursued in two core and complementing areas, can reap benefit for the student. However, there is no dearth of opportunities for single MBA specialisation as well and the choice lies with the aspirants.
