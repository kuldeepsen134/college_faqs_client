MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management

MBA / PGDM in Healthcare & Hospital Management has gained immense popularity in the past few years. With India looking to improve its healthcare facilities, management of hospitals and healthcare facilities has become prominent in this sector.

With the people getting more concerned and cautious about personal health, the focus is also shifting on the prevention than simply on cure. This change has created a newer healthcare delivery model that is more personalized and has a customer-centric approach. A range of jobs and career opportunities has come up for non-medico professionals.

The MBA / PGDM in Healthcare & Hospital Management programmes offers deep understanding of healthcare industry. FAQs – MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management

 

Q: Is a job in health administration a good choice?

Ans: For those seeking hard, meaningful work in a growing sector, healthcare administration is a wonderful opportunity. Healthcare administration is one of the nation's fastest-growing professions, with high salaries and plenty of job prospects for individuals eager to advance their careers.

 

Q: What are the career prospects after MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management?

Ans: There are multiple job opportunities available after pursuing MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management. Some of the available job profiles are marketing manager, product manager, hospital administrator, medical practice manager, pharmaceutical sales, business lead, HR executive and health insurance executive among others.

 

Q: Which are the top companies that hire MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management graduates?

Ans: Top companies that hire MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management graduates include, Cipla, Pfizer, Glenmark, Practo, Gocci, Home-Health, Portea, Bajaj Allianz, Star Health, Johnson and Johnson, Fortis and Apollo among others.

 

Q: Is MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management course offered in India?

Ans: Even tough MBA / PGDM in Healthcare & Hospital Management is a niche specialization, it is now offered by 500 plus colleges in India. Some of the top B-schools offering it are Goa Institute of Management, Goa, KJ Somaiya Institute of Management, Mumbai, Welingkar Institute of Management, Mumbai, D Y Patil University School of Management, Pune, Alliance School of Business, Bangalore.

 

Q: What is the difference between Healthcare Management and Healthcare Administration?

Ans: Healthcare administration is all about taking care of the hospital staff and employees. This department is in charge of hiring and training of staff, ensuring well being and appropriate treatment of patients and maintenance of the healthcare facility. Healthcare Management, on the other hand, includes decisions related to staff hiring, patient treatment and functioning of all other departments.

 

Q: Is it mandatory to have MBBS degree to pursue MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management?

A: No, MBBS degree is not mandatory to pursue MBA / PGDM in Healthcare & Hospital Management, but any prior knowledge is always welcome and is an advantage. However, a degree in B. Sc, BPT, B. Pharm, BTech, BDS, etc. is also widely accepted by colleges offering the course.

 

Q: What is the eligibility criteria for MBA in Healthcare Management?

A: The basic eligibility criteria for MBA / PGDM in Healthcare & Hospital Management is graduation or equivalent with minimum aggregate of 50 per cent from a recognised university (45 per cent marks in case of reserved category student). The graduation degree should be in B. Sc, MBBS, BPT, B Pharm, BTech, BDS, etc.

 

Q: What is the fee range of MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management in India?

Ans: The fees of private colleges is on the higher side in comparision to the fees of government colleges. The fee range of MBA / PGDM in Healthcare & Hospital Management in India is between Rs 2 lac to 20 lac.

 

Q: Which are the top entrance exams for MBA in Healthcare & Hospital Management?

Ans: The top entrance exams for MBA / PGDM in Healthcare & Hospital Management are CAT, XAT, SNAP, NMAT, MAT, CMAT, and GMAT.
