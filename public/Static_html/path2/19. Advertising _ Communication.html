MBA in Advertising & Communication

The primary job of an advertising professional is to convey messages to people and initiate them to buy products/ services. Advertising courses are offered under the umbrella of the mass communication domain and includes study of Public Relations and Communications. All these work together as a medium of communication with potential consumers of a product or service.

FAQs – MBA in Advertising & Communication

 

Q: What are the various modes of advertising course?

Ans: Advertising aspirants can pursue course related to advertising in UG, PG and certificate modes.  So it is available in full time, part time, distance, online as well as certificate based programs.

 

Q: What qualification is required to pursue an Advertising degree course?

Ans: Candidates who wish for a UG course in advertising like BBA in Advertising, BA in Advertising must have completed Class 12 with a minimum of 45 per cent. For PG degree / diploma courses majoring in Advertising, candidates must have completed a bachelor's degree course or equivalent in any discipline with minimum 50 per cent marks (45 per cent marks for reserved category students).

 

Q: Is advertising a good career option?

Ans: Advertising is a lucrative, stable and charming career option. On ground, Mobile, Print, Digital and TV advertising has been on a growth and hence candidates can find several job opportunities in the advertising industry.

Q: What is the fee of MBA in Advertising & Communication?

A: Colleges offering MBA / PGDM in Advertising & Communication are limited. Fees range of the colleges is between Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 10 Lac.

 

Q: Does Google Ads certification provide in-depth knowledge of Advertisement?

Ans: Google Ads certification is a professional accreditation offered by Google to impart knowledge on how ad network works on google. Candidates who wish for an advertising certification course to learn basic concepts of digital advertisement space can pursue Google Ads certification.

 

Q: What is the salary of fresher’s after completing MBA in Advertising & Communication?

Ans: A fresher’s salary after completing MBA in Advertising & Communication ranges between Rs. 4 – 5 Lacs per annum. However, with experience advertising industry is very lucarative.

 

Q: What is the difference between MBA in Marketing and MBA in Advertising & Communication?

Ans: MBA in Marketing is a broad subject with focus on multiple aspects of marketing such as market research, product development, brand alignment, etc. MBA in Advertising & Communication primarily deals with the promotion of a product, service, or brand.
