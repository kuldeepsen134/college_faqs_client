MBA in Supply Chain & Logistics Management

MBA in Supply Chain & Logistics Management involves the management of the entire supply chain of goods and services. This includes the flow of goods and services from supplier to manufacturer to wholesaler to retailer to consumer starting from raw materials till the delivery of finish goods.

MBA in Supply Chain & Logistics Management educates on how companies can deliver products to the consumer faster, in a cost effective manner and quality controlled. It touches on the aspects of aspects of logistics that’s refers to the overall process of managing how resources are acquired, stored and transported to their final destination.

FAQs – MBA in Supply Chain & Logistics Management

 

Q: What is the advantage of studying MBA in Supply Chain & Logistics Management?

Ans: With the growth in manufacturing enterprises, Supply Chain & Logistics management courses have gained popularity in India. The course imparts knowledge to build different supply chain management strategies and methods to forecast demand, aggregate planning and network design for businesses. MBA / PGDM in Supply Chain & Logistics Management professionals are in demand nowadays in production and sales companies, retail chain operators, e-commerce to name a few.

 

Q: What is the admission criterion for MBA in Supply Chain & Logistics Management?

Ans: The eligibility criteria is similar to any other specialization in MBA / PGDM, a candidate must be having minimum 50 per cent marks in aggregate in graduation (45 per cent for reserved category student).

 

Q: Is supply chain management difficult to study?

Ans: None of the specializations in MBA are difficult to study. A student must look into area of interest, career plan and academics background before deciding the specialisation. Supply chain and logistics management is both demanding and rewarding. Supply Chain Management places you at the centre of the business operations, allowing you to handle everything from packaging to shipment.

 

Q: What are the career prospects after MBA in Supply Chain & Logistics Management?

Ans: There are various career options after an MBA / PGDM in Supply Chain & Logistics Management. Some of the careers are Logistician, Supply Chain Manager, Quality Manager, Operations Analyst, Transport Officer, Resource Manager, etc.

 

Q: Which are the top colleges for MBA in Supply Chain & Logistics Management?

Q: What is the average starting salary for an MBA in Supply Chain & Logistics Management?

Ans: For a fresher graduate in MBA / PGDM in Supply Chain & Logistics Management can be Rs. 5 Lac per annum. With experience in 3 – 5 years a candidate can hike it up to Rs. 10 – 15 Lac per annum.

 

Q: What are key roles of a Supply Chain & Logistics Manager?

Ans: The key role of a Supply Chain & Logistics Manager is management of the entire supply chain of goods and services. This includes the flow of goods and services from supplier to manufacturer to wholesaler to retailer to consumer starting from raw materials till the delivery of finish goods.

Ans: Companies across the sector recruit Supply Chain & Logistics Management graduates as produict and services movement is an integral part of any industry. Some of the top recruiters are Accenture, Flipkart, Amazon, Snapdeal, Future Retail, Bharti Airtel, Reliance Industries, ITC, L & T, Proctor & Gamble, etc.

 

Q: Which are the top entrance exams for MBA / PGDM in Supply Chain & Logistics Management?

Ans: The top entrance exams for MBA / PGDM in Supply Chain & Logistics Management are CAT, XAT, SNAP, NMAT, MAT, CMAT, and GMAT.
