MBA in Rural Management

Approximately 70 percent of India’s 135 crore population lives in rural areas. It is predicted that more than 50 percent of India’s marketing, finance and human resources management programmes will require more and more qualified professionals with MBA / PGDM in Rural Management courses. This is the reason that more and more multinationals are attracted towards the skilled professionals with MBA / PGDM in Rural Management to cater to the needs of huge potential of rural market in India.

MBA / PGDM in Rural Management course includes study of various aspects like Cooperative Management, Social Management, Development Management, Rural Projects Management, Public Policy Management, etc. The course also include field-work for a considerable period of time, hence students must know that this programme is all about experiential learning.

FAQs – MBA in Rural Management

 

Q: What is Rural Management?

Ans: Rural Management can be termed as the study of management and utilization of resources available in rural arears and agriculture sector. MBA / PGDM program offered with this specialisation is called MBA / PGDM in Rural Management.

 

Q. What is the difference between MBA Rural Management & MBA Agri-business Management?

Ans: The basics of MBA in Rural Management and MBA in Agribusiness Management are more or less same as both the programmes focus on Agriculture and Rural Market Development. The main difference is that the Agri-business management gives you comprehensive learning on how to manage and develop agriculture & food production, green houses, vertical farming, roof top farming techniques that can be done in rural as well urban areas and the Rural management is about everything related agriculture business, marketing, rural land management including planning development on  rural lands, habitat protection, environmental protection.

 

Q: What is the scope of Rural Management?

Ans: The scope of Rural Management is very wide both in India and globally. A candidate can pursue a career in various sectors such as agriculture, farming, harvesting, production, manufacturing, dairy, banking, cooperative, NGO, infrastructure, technology, human resource, etc related to rural and agriculture market.

 

Q: What are the courses available in Rural Management?

Ans: Under-graduate, post-graduate and Doctoral courses are available to study rural management. One can pursue BA / BSC / BBA, MBA / PGDM and Ph D.

 

 Q: What is the eligibility criterion for MBA in Rural Management?

Ans: The eligibility criteria is similar to any other specialization, a candidate must be having minimum 50 per cent marks in aggregate in graduation (45 per cent for reserved category student).

 

Q: What is the admission procedure for MBA / PGDM in Rural Management course?

Ans: The admission procedure for MBA in Rural Management is same as regular MBA. A candidate has to appear for entrance exam such as IRMASAT, CAT, XAT, MAT, CMAT, ATMA, etc. Upon clearing the cut-off, candidate has to appear for personal interview and group discussion.

 

Q: What is the average fees of an MBA in Rural Management course?

Ans: The fees ranges between Rs. 1 Lac for government colleges to Rs. 15 Lac for private colleges.

 

Q: Which are the top colleges for MBA in Rural Management?

Ans: The top colleges for MBA in Rural Management includes IRMA, IIM Lucknow, XISS, Xavier School of Rural Management, KIIT School of Rural Management, ISMR Pune, (more names of our  tie up colleges to be written)

 

Q: Is it mandatory to have a degree in agriculture or rural in graduation for pursuing MBA / PGDM in Rural Management?

Ans: No, it is not compulsory to have a degree in rural or agriculture in graduation for MBA / PGDM in Rural Management. However, it is an added advantage for the students from similar background.

 

Q: Which are the top recruiters for MBA in Rural Management?

Ans: Top recruiters for MBA / PGDM in Rural Management degree holders include Amul, National Dairy Development Board (NDDB), ITC Limited, Tata Steel Ltd - Agrico, Tubes and Wires, NABARD, Mahindra Agri Solutions, ADM Agro Industries, Mapro Foods, Mother Dairy Fruit & Vegetable Pvt Ltd, Pidilite Industries Ltd, etc.
