MBA in International Business

MBA / PGDM in International Business deals with the nuances of international finance, e-commerce and trade policy. Companies developing businesses across countries have created a diverse and competitive workforce that can run businesses internationally.  Such companies require candidates that are trained to deal with international scenarios and are knowledgeable about the concepts and theories that can be helpful for business’ establishment.

Global MNCs and Indian MNCs like TATAs, BIRLAs, Mahindras, and IT Companies like Infosys, TCS are seeking MBA / PGDM in International Business graduates who understand international business and markets well. During an MBA / PGDM in International Business program, students study aspects of global competition, simplifying the complexities of international business management, managing international finance, global consultancy, international marketing, making best use of international logistics & supply chain, international laws on customs, tariffs, duties, documentation among others.

FAQs – MBA in International Business

 

Q: What is MBA in International business?

Ans: MBA International Business Syllabus builds a full understanding of evolving international business scenarios and techniques. The coursework includes extensive theoretical and practical sessions about country's foreign trade management and increase exports and imports. At the end of the course, the students will have an understanding of business ethics and trade policy of various countries along with techniques to enhance business.

 

Q: What is the eligibility criterion for MBA in International Business?

Ans: The eligibility criterion for MBA / PGDM in International Business is graduation or equivalent from a recognised university. Most of the institutes have minimum score criteria of 50 per cent in graduation (45 per cent for reserved category).

 

Q: Which are the top colleges in India for MBA in International Business?

Ans: Top colleges offering MBA / PGDM in International Business are Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT), Symbiosis Institute of International Business (SIIB), Pune, MDI, Gurgaon, K J Somaiya Institute of Management Studies & Research, Mumbai, BIMTECH, Great Noida (other names to be put of our choice)

 

Q: What is the admission process of MBA in International Business?

Ans: Admission process of MBA / PGDM in any top B-school is similar. Student has to clear the cut-off of entrance exam and this is followed by personality assessment through GD/WAT and PI.

 

Q: Can I pursue MBA in International Business in part-time or any other mode?

Ans: Yes, MBA in International Business can be pursued in part-time and distance learning mode. It is best suited for student who are already in job.

 

 

Q: What is the average fee of MBA in International Business course?

Ans: Fees is highly college dependent also depends upon the college being a government or a private college. The fee ranges from Rs. 1 Lac for in government colleges to to Rs. 20 lac in private colleges.

 

Q: Which jobs profiles are offered after completing MBA in International Business?

Ans: Managerial job roles available after completing MBA in / PGDM in International Business are Manager - International Business, Manager - International Finance, Manager - International Operations, Manager - Export & Imports, Manager - International Logistics, Manager - International Trade.

 

Q: What is the average salary of an International Marketing Manager in India?

Ans: The salary of an International Marketing Manager could be around Rs. 7 lac per annum, however remuneration increases as one gains work experience.

 

Q: What is the MBA in International Business syllabus?

Ans: The structure of MBA / PGDM in International Business is the same as other MBA courses. The first year comprises core subjects and from from the second year, the specialised course is taught along with the elective subjects. Some of the subjects taught are International Trade Operations & Documentation, International Marketing Management, International Economics, International Trade Logistics & Sourcing, International Business Strategy, International Financial Management, International Contract Management, Management of Shipping Services, Agri-Commodity Trading (ACT), Managing Global Compliance in Exports, Customs Procedure in GST Regime, Risk Management in International Trade, Sectoral Strategies for Exports, Global Leadership Strategies, International Human Resource Management Practices, International Investment Treaties & Business Decisions.

 

Q: Do colleges offering full time MBA / PGDM in International Business provide campus placement?

Ans: Yes, top colleges do provide excellent campus placement. Some of the top recruiters are HDFC Bank, Citi Bank, Amazon, ICICI Bank, HSBC, Google, Axis Bank, IBM, Accenture, Cognizant, WIPRO, Crisil, JP Morgan Chase & Co., Bain & Company, KPMG, McKinsey & Company, etc.

 

Q: Which entrance exam score is required for admission to MBA in International Business course?

Ans: For admission to MBA in International Business courses offered by colleges in India, candidates can appear for CAT, GMAT, IIFT, XAT, CMAT and MAT.
