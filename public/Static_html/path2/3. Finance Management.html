MBA in Finance

MBA / PGDM in Finance is one of the most preferred specialization which provides skills in Financial Management, Corporate Financial Planning, Corporate Acquisitions & Mergers, Accounting, and Banking. MBA / PGDM Finance syllabus is designed in such a way that it enables the students to analyse the Financial Reports, Forecast financial and economic trends, making stock profitable, making a balance approach between risk and profitability, managing and maximizing investment portfolio with minimum risk factor.

MBA in Finance degree equips an individual with the ability, knowledge and techniques to get employment in corporate as well as government sector. MBA in Finance graduates can also get jobs in stock markets, audit agencies and taxation firms. They can also set up their own business.

FAQs – MBA in Finance

Q. What is the career scope after MBA / PGDM in Finance?

Ans: Every business organisation need finance professionals and hiring is done at junior, middle and senior level to take care of the finance and financial assets of the company. MBA / PGDM in Finance will open the door for jobs at executive as well as managerial positions in good companies. After completion a candidate can get a job as a Financial Advisor, Financial Planner, Budget Analyst etc.

 

Q.  Who is eligible to pursue MBA / PGDM in Finance?

Ans: Anyone with a legitimate graduation degree in any discipline with minimum 50 per cent marks is eiligible to pursue MBA / PGDM in Finance. For reserved category students, the minimum mark is 45 per cent.

 

Q: Is Maths necessary for MBA in Finance?

Ans: Maths is an integral part of all MBA / PGDM specialisation. For MBA / PGDM in Finance, good command on Maths is an advantage as the course includes some topics of Accounting, Taxation, Economics and Finance.

 

Q. Is MBA / PGDM in Finance difficult as compared to other specializations?

Ans: It totally depends upon the student perspective. Some may find HR or Marketing easier and opt for the same, while for others MBA / PGDM in Finance is a better and easier choice to study.

 

Q. What is the course fee of MBA / PGDM in Sales and Finance?

Ans: MBA / PGDM in Finance is offered as a specialization in B-schools. The course fee for entire 2 years ranges from Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 25 Lac.

 

Q. What is the average salary of a fresher after completing MBA / PGDM in Finance?

Ans: The average salary of MBA / PGDM in Finance ranges between Rs. 5 Lac to Rs. 25 Lac per annum.

 

Q: What is the admission process for MBA / PGDM in Finance course?

Ans: Admission process is mostly dependent on the B-school a student is targeting. In general the admission processed comprises of entrance test followed up by group discussion and personal interview.

 

Q: MBA in Finance or MBA in Marketing, which specialisation has more scope?

Ans: Both marketing and finance are very popular and have a lot of scope, as they are two of the most important department in any industry, sector or organization. The candidate must choose either of the two specializations or any other specializations of their choice based on their skills, interest and future goal.

 

Q: Which are the top colleges for MBA in Finance in India?

Ans: Top colleges in India for MBA / PGDM in Finance include S P Jain Institute of Management and Research (SPJIMR), Mumbai, Institute of Management Technology (IMT), Ghaziabad, Jamnalal Bajaj Institute of Management Studies (JBIMS), Mumbai, K J Somaiya Institute of Management Studies & Research, Mumbai, (more names of our  tie up colleges to be written)

 

Q: What do you study in MBA / PGDM in Finance?

Ans: Apart from the core subjects students in Finance, a student has to study specialisation centric subjects like Money and Banking, Financial Risk Management, Management Control System, International Finance, Bank Management, Fixed Income Markets, Structured Finance, Infrastructure and Project Finance, Options, Futures & Derivatives, Business Valuation, Investment Analysis and Portfolio Management, Corporate Restructuring, Behavioural Finance, Trading Strategies etc.

 

Q: Which are the top companies for recruitment for MBA / PGDM in Finance?

Ans: Hiring is done at all levels for a finance graduate. Some of the top recruiters for MBA / PGDM in Finance include Morgan Stanley, Deutsche Bank, Deloitte, E & Y, KPMG, McKinsey, Barclays, Boston Consulting Group, Goldman Sachs etc.

 

Q: Which are the top entrance exams for MBA / PGDM in Finance?

Ans: The top entrance exams for MBA in Finance are CAT, XAT, SNAP, NMAT, MAT, CMAT, and GMAT.
