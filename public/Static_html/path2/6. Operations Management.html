MBA in Operations Management

The term Operations Management comprises planning, implementing, and also supervising the production of goods and management and also looking after the management. The professionals working in this field have to look after the production as well as management of the goods and services.

An MBA in operations management provides a global perspective on industry trends that can help an individual meet client demand. With the rise of E-commerce, MBA Operations Management is becoming more significant.

FAQs – MBA in Operations Management

 

Q: What is MBA / PGDM in Operations Management?

Ans: MBA / PGDM in Operations Management is a two-year post graduate course. Operation forms the backbone of a business enterprise as it is primarily about product / service launch and management. The Operations department of an organisations takes care of the technology and processes involved in production or goods and services.

 

Q: What is the admission criterion for MBA in Operations?

Ans: The eligibility criteria is similar to any other specialization for Operations Management, a candidate must be having minimum 50 per cent marks in aggregate in graduation (45 per cent for reserved category student).

 

Q: Which course is better MBA in Operations or MBA in IT & Systems?

Ans: MBA / PGDM in Operations and MBA / PGDM in IT & Systems are specialized programs and both are very popular in the job market as the businesses have grown to solely depend upon information technology. Thus, one can choose to pursue either of the courses, depending on interest and academic background.

 

Q: What are the career prospects after MBA in Operations?

Ans: MBA / PGDM in Operations opens managerial level openings in industries such as e-commerce, information technology, consulting, FMCG and BFSI. One can get job roles such as Operations Manager, Product Manager, Team Lead, Consultant, etc.

 

Q: Which are the top colleges for MBA in Operations Management?

Ans: Apart from IIM’s, top colleges in India for MBA / PGDM in Operations Management include S P Jain Institute of Management and Research (SPJIMR), Mumbai, Institute of Management Technology (IMT), Ghaziabad, Jamnalal Bajaj Institute of Management Studies (JBIMS), Mumbai, K J Somaiya Institute of Management Studies & Research, Mumbai, (more names of our  tie up colleges to be written)

 

Q: What is the average starting salary in Operations Management roles?

Ans: The average starting salary in Operations Manager is Rs 6 – 7 lac per annum. If a candidate pursue MBA / PGDM in Operations from a Tier 1 colleges, package can go up to Rs 14 - 23 lac per annum salary.

 

Q: What is the eligibility criteria for pursuing MBA in Operations?

Ans: The eligibility criteria for pursuing MBA in Operations is graduation in any stream or equivalent from a recognised university. However, at some colleges, MBA / PGDM in Operations is reserved for engineering and technology background students.

 

Q: Can I pursue MBA in Operations after doing job for a few years?

Ans: One can pursue both full time MBA or executive MBA after doing job for a few years. If the experience is of more than 2 – 3 years then the student is eligible for executive MBA.

 

Q: What are key roles of an Operations Manager?

 

A: The key roles of an Operations Manager is to plan, supervise, train, manage quality assurance programmes and strategize process improvements. They are responsible for maintaining and increasing productivity at minimal cost. 

Q: Which are the top companies that hire Operations Management graduates?

 

A: Companies across the sector hire Operations Management graduates in large number at handsome pay packages. Some of the top companies that hire MBA in Operations Management include Amazon India, Flipkart, Myntra, Accenture, L&T, JP Morgan Chase, KPMG, Wipro, Tata Consultancy Services Limited, Adani Group, Mind Tree, Adecco, etc.
