Part Time MBA

Part Time MBA is a program that can be done in parallel with your job or any other regular course by attending classes on weekends or evenings classes. It is specially designed for working professionals who cannot take time off work to attend full-time courses.  This programme is particularly appealing to individuals who wish to progress in their existing careers. It is a 2-year postgraduate program in which there are 4-semesters of 6 months each, but options are there to complete in a longer period also.

Best Part-Time MBA Programmes in India are offered by institutes including MDI, IMT, Symbiosis, NMIMS amongst others. Besides, Part-Time MBA from IIMs has also strengthened the concept of doing Part-Time MBA Courses.

 

Highlights of Part Time MBA

Part-Time MBA stands for part-time Master of Business Administration completed in a total duration of two to three years with evening and weekend classes. The programme is aimed to equip working professionals with proper management skills.

Why Part Time MBA?

Part-time MBA program is best suited for those who want to earn an MBA degree while doing their job. The best part of doing a part-time MBA is that there is no time boundation and there is flexibility for attending classes. Like every coin, there are its own perks and lows associated with a Part-time MBA.

Advantages  - Part Time MBA

If you are a working professional, have less time, wish to gain more by spending less, want to have career growth within the organization, the part time MBA is a good proposition for you:

 

·         Class Timing is Flixible : Classes are held on weekends & outside working hours on weekdays, thus part time MBA program is more flexible as compared to full time MBA.

·         Low Fee and Earning Opportunity: Fee for Part time MBA is low as compared to full time MBA & since you are mostly pursuing Part Time MBA along with the Job or Business, you also get to earn while pursuing part time MBA.

·         Exposure: Since you are pursuing MBA along with the Job, you get to gain work experience, practical exposure and also polish your managerial skills.

·         Career Growth: Part-time MBA program, lets you evaluate whether the career growth you are expecting is in accordance with the demand of the industry or not, and helps you develop the necessary skills, all this while you continue your job.

·         Learning: Faculty for Part Time MBA and Full Times MBA are same in an institute, hence learning experience is similar with full time MBA.

Disadvantages  - Part Time MBA

Although, MBA or equivalent qualification puts a candidate into fast-track job profiles, but you may lose interest in doing MBA due to the longer duration of Part time MBA, may feel tired after working hours, may not get benefits of peer learning and above all the top corporates may not give any weightage to the part time MBA.

·         Longer Duration: Part time MBA is spread over duration of 2 - 3 years; the duration of the program is longer as compared to 2 year full time MBA or One Year Executive MBA and this makes you lose interest in long term.

·         Exhaustive: Working while pursuing part time MBA can be exhaustive, monotonous, & tedious to balance both the things at the same time.

·         Lack of On-Campus Experience: Despite having the same curriculum students also lose out on the productive classroom sessions and interactive discussions with peers.

·         Reputation within Corporates: Generally a full time MBA is more preferable as compared to part time MBA when recruiting candidates with similar roles & profile.

 

FAQs – Part-Time MBA

Q: For who is the Part-time MBA best suited?

Ans: Part-time MBA is best suited for working professionals who cannot afford to invest the requisite time and funds required for the full-time MBA or Executive MBA. By attending evening or weekend classes they can continue with their jobs alongside upgrading their skills and having the MBA degree.

 

 

Q: How is a Part-time MBA better in comparison to the full-time MBA / Executive MBA or Distance MBA programs?

Ans: Full-time MBA program requires 100% participation and most of the programs and residential ones and Distance MBA program is offered in an online or correspondence mode wherein direct communication with the faculty members or fellow students is minimal. The Part-Time MBA module takes care of both these aspects.

Part Time MBA is more affordable, imbibes the learning in a classroom environment, offers a pedagogy that is a mix of traditional and modern methods. It also gives students an opportunity to interact with the professors, attend guest lecturers from industry experts and peer group interaction.

 

Q: What is the duration of a Part-time MBA?

Ans: The duration of a Part-time MBA is flexible. Students can complete the programme as per their convenience and capabilities within three to five years. Each institute has its specific duration and some of them also allow students to take an extension to complete the program.

 

Q: Is the curriculum of a Part-time MBA as rigorous as a full-time MBA?

Ans: Part-time MBA / PGDM is divided into two years or three years and each year consist of two semesters. The syllabus of the course is almost similar to that of a full-time programme. The only difference is that in part-time MBA / PGDM, the subjects are taught in a more flexible manner hence less rigorous. The programme comprises core subject like Business Law, Managerial Economics, Competition and Strategy, Macroeconomics, Corporate Finance, Corporate Governance and Ethics, Marketing Management etc. The electives options available are Corporate Strategy and Policy, Economics and Social Sciences, Entrepreneurial Learning, Decision Sciences and Information Systems, Organisational Behaviour and HRM, Accounting and Finance etc.

 

Q: Is Part Time MBA with specialisation much better?

A: This is a choice that a student should take based on his/her career ambition or goal. If s/he aims to work in a particular sector, then it is best to choose a suitable specialisation. On the other hand, if the student wants to excel in the present job and get the management lessons on each function to prep up for a leadership role, then a general Part-time MBA would suffice. Q: Is there an age limit for pursuing a Part-time MBA program?

Ans: There is no defined age limit, however, some of the institutes do have an age limit criteria, which ranges from 27 years to 50 years.

 

Q: What kind of industry exposure can a student expect from Part-time MBA courses?

Ans: Since the students are working professionals, they are already aware of the major functions and operations involved in an organization, therefore there is no scope of industry training or visits. However, colleges do arrange for guest lecturers from industry experts.

 

Q: Are placement processes conducted after the completion of the program?

Ans: Placement process is not conducted by the institutes after the program completion as students are working professionals. However, some institutes do provide alumni based placed assistance.

 

Q: What are the scopes for a student after completing the program?

Ans: Since the candidates are working professionals, the scope lies in getting promoted to a higher managerial post. In case he/she wants to change the career path or explore a more challenging leadership role in a different industry type or organisation in the same sector, the Part-time MBA would be the roadmap to the new avenues and sectors.

 

Q: Is a Part-time MBA considered relevant by the industry?

Ans: Yes, there is equal acceptance of the Part-time MBA holders as there is for the full-time MBA or Executive MBAs. Moreover, if pursued from one of the top B-schools, the Part-time MBA is aptly valued by the industry. The prior work experience is also considered. Rather than the degree, the companies tend to look at the skillsets, leadership qualities and ability to control a crisis or turn challenges into opportunities of the existing or prospective employees.
