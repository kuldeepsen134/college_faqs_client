MBA and PGDM Admission Process

MBA and PGDM admission in all the top MBA and PGDM colleges across India are categoriesed in 5 steps. These 5 steps of admission process are applicable to all the MBA and PGDM colleges including the IIMs who offer admission after a rigorous selection process to select the most suitable candidate.

1: Register & Apply for Entrance Exam

The admission process in top MBA and PGDM colleges normally opens in the first week of August / September and closes by December end every year. Candidates desirous of seeking admission in top colleges should register and apply for the relevant entrance exam like CAT / XAT / IIFT / NMAT / SNAP / MAT within the specified registration window.

National level MBA and PGDM entrance exams like CAT, XAT etc normally close the registration process between September to November. Usually the CAT registration process remains open for 45 to 50 days.

2: Appear in Entrance Exam

Candidates who want to pursue management course PGDM / MBA programme from a top college must appear in CAT, XAT, NMAT or other relevant entrance exam. It could be a National, State or Institute level entrance exam.

3: Results and Cut-off

Results of most of the exams are declared by January. B-schools will weigh the scores and each college will release its minimum cut-off marks based on the entrance exams accepted for admissions.  It’s to be noted that examination score is valid for 1 year only.

4. Apply for College / Institution

Candidates clearing the cut-off score of the desired college and program, and then must apply for the respective college for its MBA and PGDM program. It is to be noted that admissions do not happen only on the basis of entrance exams score. Colleges also consider other parameters such as academics, work experience and selection rounds. So students can apply for a top college basis low entrance exam score also.

5: Shortlisting Process

Shortlisting process is is completed by each institute individually basis its internal selection parameters. Shortlisted candidates are called for various selection rounds such as Group Discussion (GD), Written Aptitude Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI). There are some other rounds also like Extempore and Psychometric Test conducted by few selected colleges. Candidates must appear for these rounds to qualify for the selection for their college and program.

6: Final Admission Process- GD-PI-WAT Round

On the basis of a candidates overall performance in final selection round, weightage is awarded to entrance scores, academic profile & diversity, work experience and gender diversity. Final merit list is generated and admission offers will be made by the respective B-school. Candidates will have to pay the admission fee to confirm their seats in the selected college.

 

FAQs on MBA and PGDM Admission

 

Q: Can I apply for MBA course now?

Ans: MBA and PGDM admissions for 2023 batch have started with announcement of registrations for many entrance exams. Many government and private colleges are inviting applications for admission on merit as well as entrance exam score basis. To check out the admission process of respective colleges, click here.

 

Q: Can I apply for MBA with below 50 per cent in Graduation?

Ans: Yes, few colleges accept students with less than 50 per cent aggregate in graduation. For SC/ST and reserved category candidates, the minimum mark required is 45 per cent in all MBA colleges.

 

Q: Is GD/PI mandatory for admission to MBA course?

Ans: It can be assumed that Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI) is mandatory, as almost all the top MBA and PGDM colleges in India conduct GD / PI for admission. Colleges conduct these rounds as it is essential to assess the candidates’ communication abilities and leadership qualities which make a successful manager.

 

 

Q: Is there any MBA and PGDM college for direct admission?

Ans: Yes, there are several MBA and PGDM colleges in India, which offer direct admission to meritorious students. Along with that there are options for Online and Distance courses without having to appear for any entrance exam. To check out such colleges, click here.

 

Q: Which are the top entrance exams for MBA?

Ans: Top entrance exams for MBA and PGDM admission are CAT, XAT, MAT, CMAT, NMAT, SNAP, ATMA, MAH CET, TANCET, TSICET, APICET, KMAT, KMAT Kerala and Karnataka PGCET etc. For details about entrance exams, click here.

 

Q: Do I need to appear for entrance exam even for Distance MBA course?

Ans: No, it is not mandatory to appear for a Distance MBA course. Most of the university/institutes offering Distance MBA offer admission on the basis of academic record.

 

Q: Is it mandatory to have work experience to pursue MBA and PGDM?

Ans: No, any kind of work experience is not mandatory to study in a top B-school to pursue MBA and PGDM program. Selection is based on other important parameters including entrance exam score, GD-WAT-PI, academics.

 

Q: What is the benefit of pursuing MBA or PGDM after gaining work experience?

Ans: Yes, there are benefits of pursuing MBA after gaining work experience, but neither it is mandatory to have work experience nor it guarantees admission in a top B-school. 

Candidates with work experience stand to score extra points during selection process. During the course, candidates will be able to grasp better and faster due to their previous work experience in the functioning of an organization. At the time of campus placements candidates having work experience stand to grab better roles and salary packages.
